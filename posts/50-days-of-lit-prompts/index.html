50 Days of LIT Prompts

Learn prompt engineering through doing with new prompt patterns every weekday for 10 weeks
7 min intro video

David Colaursso
First Published January XX, 2024 (updated every weekday)

Generative AI is changing so fast it's an open question if prompt engineering will still be a thing in 10 weeks, but let's give this a go. Just so you know, I've always been a little susspecious of "prompt engineering" as an endevor. The common framing presents it as the no-code skill of AI wisperer. Real engineering requiers repeatbility

Questions or comments? I'm on Mastodon @Colarusso@mastodon.social

Week 1

  1. Summarize and question the contents of a webpage from within the browser
  2. Define a selected word or phrase on a webpage
  3. Define a selected word or phrase given the context of the webpage on which it is found
  4. Use the text of an email to draft a polite reply declining any request(s)
  5. Turn the outcome of a coin flip into a poem

Week 2

  1. For a webpage with a click-bait headline, return the author's answer to the question posed by the headline absent extranious verbage
  2. For an online "reciepe,"" return the reciepe absent extranious verbage
  3. Find the "asks" in an email, produce a bulletted list of same, and add them to a to do list saved on your scratch pad
  4. Save the contents of your scratch pad to a file
  5. Produce a selection of click-bait headlines based on the text of your scratch pad

Week 3

  1. Craft Simple Training Simulations with an "AI Actor"
  2. Diagram selected sentence, return JSON
  3. Complete a "form letter" without engaging an LLM
  4. Translate and reply in original language
  5. Turn the time of day in to a poem

Week 4

  1. Play Devil's Advocate
  2. Provide feedback on style selected text
  3. Rewrite selected text in plain language
  4. Shorten text
  5. Expand text through dialoug with an AI writing assistant

Week 5

  1. Simulate the socratic method
  2. Create multipe choice questions for a reading
  3. Create true-false questions for a reading
  4. Answer multiple choice questions
  5. Answer true-false questions

Week 6

  1. Answer multiple choice questions given a knowledge base
  2. Answer true-false questions given a knowledge base
  3. Answer questions based on a FAQ
  4. Answer a question based on a set of FAQs
  5. BS with a Bot

Week 7

  1. Blurb a webpage for social media
  2. Read a webpage and flag any logical falacies
  3. Translate an angry comment into thoughtful statment in context (inject original comment anger translator)
  4. Conduct an interview
  5. Fun "Facts": This day in history

Week 8

  1. Help me write an outline
  2. Create a customized AI Word Processor. outline flesh out edit suggestions
  3. Create a customized Summ and Question for multiple papers
  4. Breakdown problem into bite-sized chunks
  5. Ability check and respond

Week 9

  1. Weekly Reflection
  2. Random simulations (diff every time)
  3. Simulations with evaluations
  4. Rephrase for three audiances: 5yo, general audiance, wonk
  5. Export a "readings" bot

Week 10

  1. Summarize and question the contents of a scratch pad (text area you can edit)
  2. Reply to email based on a set of options
