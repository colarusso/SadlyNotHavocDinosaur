50 Days of LIT Prompts

Learn prompt engineering through doing with new prompt patterns every weekday for 10 weeks
Let's Build Something, latent space "photography" by Colarusso

David Colaursso
Published 2024-01-29 (updated every weekday through 2024-04-08)

As an experiential educator, I firmly believe experience is an excelent teacher. So, let's build something. In the process we might just gain some perspective on this whole generative AI craze.

I teach law students about technology, and recently I created the LIT Prompts Browser extension to help my students explore the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) and prompt engineering. You can download it here: Firefox or Chrome.

Every business day for the next 10 weeks, I'll update this page with new prompt patterns and invite folks to play with them inside LIT Prompts. We'll be plugging into the technology that runs tools like ChatGPT, giving you a level of control that most people don't see. You won't have to do any "coding," but we will think like coders. I'm highly suspcious of the term prompt engineering, esp. when used as a job title. The idea that an entire job could be built around "talking" to an AI seems a bit too loosey goosy. So, we'll try to introduce some riggor. We'll aim to think about workflows and systems where writing prompts is just part of a larger endevor. You'll be supported by the fact that LIT Prompts lets you produce reusable interconecting prompt templats. This will let us build upon what we learn and improve our practice over time. We'll explore the promise and periles of this new technology while you build custom interactions/workflows. Plus, I'm pretty sure we'll have fun along the way.
